About this place

Designated in 1965 as the eighth Illinois Nature Preserve, the 245 acres of Salt Creek Woods hold a remnant of what most of the region looked like before the city did: oak savanna, oak-hickory woodland, and floodplain forest along a winding creek. It is among the oldest intact habitats in Cook County, kept that way by sixty years of active stewardship — proof that conservation, not just creation, is part of the design work.

What you're hearing

A brilliant orange Baltimore Oriole sings amongst many other songbirds, including Tennessee Warbler, Red-winged Blackbird, Yellow Warbler, American Goldfinch, American Robin, Blue Jay, Cedar Waxwing, and Wood Thrush.
