About this place

Sixty miles south of downtown, Midewin (mid-DAY-win, a Potawatomi word meaning "Grand Medicine Society") is a 20,000-acre tallgrass prairie under restoration on the site of the former Joliet Army Arsenal. Once a TNT plant, it is now the largest protected open space in the Chicago region and the largest tallgrass prairie restoration east of the Mississippi — a landscape rewriting itself across decades.

What you're hearing

A diversity of sparrows fills the tallgrass, including the threatened grassland specialists Henslow's Sparrow and Le Conte's Sparrow, alongside White-crowned Sparrows and others. These birds are on their way south, and will return to Chicago the following spring.
