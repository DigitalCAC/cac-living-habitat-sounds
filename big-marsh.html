About this place

On Chicago's southeast side, where Acme Steel's slag once filled the wetland to depths of 15 feet, 297 acres now function again as marsh. Big Marsh Park opened in 2016 after decades of remediation, capping contaminated land and replanting native cover. More than 240 bird species have been recorded here. The site is a working argument that even our most damaged ground can be brought back into ecological function.

What you're hearing

The full sounds of spring fill the marsh: American Robin, Yellow Warbler, Red-winged Blackbird, Eastern Kingbird, Warbling Vireo, Northern Parula, and other warblers. Listen toward the end — a passing train rouses what may be a chorus of coyotes.
