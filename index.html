Audio recordings from the region's distinct ecosystems.

Five places. Five mornings. Five soundscapes from the region's distinct ecosystems—a restored tallgrass prairie, the Chicago lakefront, an urban rooftop, a remnant woodland, and a former industrial dumping ground turned marsh.

In the city and beyond, many of these places are reachable by foot, bike, bus, or train. They invite exploration, deepen our connection to nature, and remind us that caring for the land also means caring for one another.

Part of Chicago's Living Habitat, on view at the Chicago Architecture Center.

The Wings Over the Windy City project uses sound recording and remote sensing to illuminate one of North America's great migration routes while identifying where birds may face the greatest risks from light pollution, buildings, and other features of the built environment. The project is led by the Migration Biology Lab at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, which studies how migratory birds respond to a changing world and works with conservation partners to support bird-safe cities and actions that help migratory birds thrive. Key partners and supporters include Walder Foundation, Windy City Bird Lab, Chicago Bird Collision Monitors, and Openlands. Learn more about the project and the Chicago Bird Migration Monitoring Network at migrationbiology.org/chicago and chicagobirdmigration.net.
